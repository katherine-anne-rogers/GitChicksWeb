Discover the Amazing Avian Diversity of London!

London, Ontario is home to a diverse array of bird species, making it a prime destination for birdwatching enthusiasts. Whether you're a seasoned birder or a curious nature lover, the Forest City has something to offer.

Previous Next
   

London is officially designated as a Bird Friendly City by Nature Canada. This means our city has worked to help protect native birds and restore their habitats. Some of these programs include education and outreach, such as teaching about the dangers of unmarked windows and outdoor cats. Nature London offers nature tours for birders of all levels of knowledge and mobility. In 2021, the Northern Cardinal was voted as the official bird of London, after already having a reputation of being the Cardinal Capital of Canada

Every year, citizen naturalists take part in the Christmas Bird Count between mid-December and early January. This is a national event that teaches us a lot about which birds spend their winters in Canada, and where they live. The data collected are used by conservationists, government and non-profit agencies, and other policy makers to help them help birds more effectively. London has participated in the count since 1909, meaning we have the longest uninterrupted count in Canada - 113 years!

One of the most popular birding spots in London is the Coves Environmentally Significant Area. Located on the Thames River in South London, this area is the breeding location for fifty-nine bird species. Nature London frequently offers birding tours through the park, featuring Great Blue Herons, Great Egrets, Killdeer, Red-bellied Woodpeckers, and many species of warbler.

Another favourite hotspot is Kilally Meadows. Located near Huron Heights, this park is full of meadows and swamps, a very different terrain than the Coves. Some common sightings here include White-breasted Nuthatches, Carolina Wrens, Cedar Waxwings, and Golden-crowned Kinglets. Kilally Meadows is also home to a network of hiking, walking, and biking trails for easy access to our feathered friends.

London is also a great city for backyard birding. This is the easiest form of bird-watching, especially if you're just starting out! Setting up a bird feeder (or two) near a kitchen window can provide you with free entertainment at every meal! You'll be greeted by Red- and White-breasted Nuthatches, Black-capped Chickadees, Northern Cardinals, and Dark-eyed Juncos. In the evening you may see House Finches, Mourning Doves, and Sparrows of every kind. This is a fun and easy way to learn more about the natural world around us, and about the flying residents of London.

If you're new to birdwatching, there are a huge number of local resources to help you get started. Nature London is your go-to for all things birds in London. By becoming a member for $35 ($10 for students!) you receive a monthly newsletter all about the happenings in London, including new and relevant scientific research, book reviews, and annual bird and butterfly counts. As well, you receive a discount at Featherfields Bird and Garden, an emporium for all things outdoors, with locations in Hyde Park and Wortley Village. They sell field guides, bird watching supplies, and bird seed and feeders, as well as home and garden decor.

Another great educational resource is Bird Friendly London. They can teach you everything you need to know about keeping your home bird-safe! They also run programs like bird seed giveaways and accessible birding tours.

Overall, London is a haven for birdwatchers and nature lovers alike. With its diverse habitats, variety of species, and abundance of resources, it's the perfect place to explore and appreciate the feathered friends in our community. We hope you'll join us on this journey to discover the amazing diversity of London, Ontario.
